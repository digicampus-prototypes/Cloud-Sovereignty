SOV Objectives & SEAL Levels — Quick Reference

Each alternative is tagged with relevant Sovereignty Objectives (SOV-1 through SOV-8) indicating where it provides meaningful assurance, plus an estimated overall SEAL level based on publicly available information about ownership, jurisdiction, data handling, supply chain, and certifications.

SOV-1 Strategic — EU ownership, governance, investment
SOV-2 Legal & Jurisdictional — EU law, no CLOUD Act exposure
SOV-3 Data & AI — Encryption, data confinement, AI governance
SOV-4 Operational — Portability, EU talent, documentation
SOV-5 Supply Chain — HW/SW origin, transparency, audit
SOV-6 Technology — Openness, interoperability, open standards
SOV-7 Security & Compliance — EU certs, SOC, GDPR, NIS2
SOV-8 Environmental — Energy, circular economy, carbon

⚠️ Assessment methodology note: The SEAL levels shown are estimated based on publicly available information about each provider's ownership structure, legal jurisdiction, data handling practices, supply chain, open-source status, and certifications. A formal SEAL assessment per the EC Cloud Sovereignty Framework v1.2.1 would require a full tender questionnaire and evidence review by the contracting authority. Swiss providers are capped at SEAL-3 because Swiss law, while GDPR-equivalent and privacy-friendly, is technically non-EU jurisdiction. Japanese providers are rated SEAL-2 due to friendly but non-EU legal frameworks. Self-hosted open-source solutions can achieve SEAL-4 when deployed on EU-owned infrastructure.
